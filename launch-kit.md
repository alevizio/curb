## 4 · Reddit r/sanfrancisco — warm-up comments (post on OTHERS' threads first, NO link)

These build account standing before/around the Juneteenth PSA so your account isn't 100% self-promo. **Do not mention curb.guide in any of these.**

**On a "got a ticket at 9:40 even though the sign says 9–11" thread:**
> Sucks, but unfortunately the sign is the rule and you were technically parked during the posted window. The catch nobody tells you: the sweeper usually comes through right when the window opens, and enforcement tends to follow it almost immediately. On most blocks the tickets cluster in the first 20-ish minutes after the start time, not spread across the full two hours. So a 9-11am sign realistically means "be gone by ~9:15," not 11. Move it before the start time and you're basically never caught. Appealing rarely works if the sign was posted, sadly.

**On a "just moved to the Mission, how do I avoid tickets" thread:**
> Welcome. Few things that'll save you real money: 1) Learn your block's street-cleaning days cold — that's the #1 ticket in the city by far, and the Mission is the single worst neighborhood for it. Cleaning mornings around your area skew early, like 8am, and Thursdays tend to be heavy. 2) Check if you're in an RPP (permit) zone and get the residential permit if so. 3) Set a recurring phone alarm the night before each sweep day. Honestly the alarm is what keeps most of us out of trouble. The signs are always the final word, so read each one when you park.

**On a "does street cleaning run on [holiday]?" thread:**
> SF suspends daytime street-sweeping enforcement on about a dozen city holidays (New Year's, Juneteenth, Labor Day, Thanksgiving, etc.), so you generally don't have to chase the sweeper those mornings. One caveat: holiday suspensions cover street cleaning, but they don't automatically cover metered parking, RPP time limits, or tow-away/peak-hour zones — those can still be enforced. SFMTA's official holiday enforcement schedule is the authority here, so it's worth a 10-second check for your specific spot. And as always, if there's a sign saying otherwise on your block, the sign wins.

**Holiday PSA (reusable):** before the next city holiday, post a text PSA (no link) — "No daytime street-sweeping enforcement on [holiday] ([date])" + the metered/RPP caveat above. Evergreen template; fill in the next holiday.
