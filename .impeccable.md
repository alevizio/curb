# CURB — Design Context

## Audience
Everyday San Francisco residents who park on the street and live in fear of the $105
street-sweeping ticket. Mobile-first, often standing on the sidewalk deciding whether
a spot is safe. Secondary: people a user texts "is this spot ok?".

## Use cases
- "Can I park here, and until when?" (map, curb colors, verdict)
- "Remind me before the sweeper comes" (.ics / Google Cal / Web Push)
- "When do tickets ACTUALLY land on this block?" (citation-derived 🎯 times — the differentiator)
- "Where can I load / is this a permit zone?" (layers)

## Brand personality / tone
**A well-designed civic notice.** Precise, quietly confident, honest to a fault
(every claim hedged to "the posted sign is the source of truth"). Personality lives in
craft and typography, not jokes. Think: DMV poster redesigned by a very good studio.
Volume: clean & trustworthy — NOT loud/cheeky (explicitly chosen 2026-06-09).

## Visual system (the "transit signage" language)
- Tokens in `:root` of index.html — paper #F2ECDF / ink #17150F / ink-soft #4A4536;
  status green/amber/red (+ darker `-text` siblings for AA text-on-paper); meter blue #2F5BD0.
- Type: **Anton** (display, uppercase, tight) + **Hanken Grotesk** (everything else). Never add a third.
- Hard offset shadows (`5px 5px 0 ink`, chips 3px), 2–3px ink borders, radius 8–22.
- Light theme only. No gradients, no glassmorphism, no dark-mode-with-glow.
- Map basemap: warm parchment (styledmap "Parchment Draft", neutralized roads).

## Constraints
- Vanilla HTML/CSS/JS, single static files, no build step, no frameworks.
- WCAG AA: use the `-text` status tokens for any colored text on paper.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on all animation.
- The app (map) stays the instant default at `/` — marketing lives at `/about`.
